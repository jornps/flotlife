technicalDetails: |
  **What it does:** Biological treatment uses microorganisms to break down dissolved organic matter — BOD, COD, nitrogen and phosphorus — that physical-mechanical treatment (screening, DAF) cannot remove. The result is effluent that meets surface-water discharge standards or qualifies for indirect discharge to a municipal sewer.

  **When it's needed:** Required when the receiving water body has a tight BOD/COD limit, when nutrient (N, P) removal is mandated, or when a direct sewer connection isn't available at the site.

  **Process configurations:**
  - Aerobic activated sludge (conventional or membrane bioreactor/MBR)
  - Attached-growth systems suited to smaller or variable flows
  - Combined aerobic/anoxic stages for nitrogen removal where required

  **Integration in the process line:** Positioned after DAF; biological effluent can be polished further with filtration or UV disinfection before final discharge. Biological sludge feeds the dewatering unit alongside flotation sludge.

  **Reference performance — Irbe Fish (Latvia, canned fish, discharge to sea):**
  - Inlet COD 2 600–3 200 mg/l → Outlet COD 18 mg/l
  - Inlet BOD 1 690–2 600 mg/l → Outlet BOD 5 mg/l
  - Inlet TSS >1 800 mg/l → Outlet TSS <3 mg/l

  **Automation:** Aeration, nutrient dosing and sludge return monitored and controlled remotely via SCADA.

Physical separation removes what floats or settles, but dissolved organic material needs
a different approach. Biological treatment uses microorganisms to break that load down,
typically as a stage after DAF, bringing effluent quality up to the standard your permit
requires. We size and configure this stage to your actual influent characteristics rather
than a one-size-fits-all design.
