Sludge collected by flotation or screening is still mostly water. Our FDS multi-disc
screw press mechanically squeezes that water out, concentrating solids into a dry,
transportable cake — reducing volume by up to 7x and cutting transport and disposal
costs sharply. Automation handles feed rate and monitoring, so the process runs
unattended between collection cycles.

The FDS line isn't limited to fixed installations. Our **FDS-MB**, a trailer-mounted
mobile version of the same press, has already been demonstrated on municipal sludge,
fish processing and biological treatment sludge, dairy flotation sludge, brewery,
ice cream and red beet processing waste, and metal, paint and oil-industry process
water — including a documented case at a cod farm in Norway. It's a practical way to
trial dewatering on your own sludge, or cover a site that doesn't justify a permanent
unit, before committing to a fixed installation. [See it in action on Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/showcase/fdsmb).

The output is also where the sustainability story starts: less to haul away, more
potential to recover as a resource. See our [sustainability page](/en/sustainability/)
for the fuller picture.
