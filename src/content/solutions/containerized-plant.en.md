technicalDetails: |
  **What's inside:** A pre-assembled containerized plant combines multiple treatment stages — typically screening, chemical conditioning, DAF, biological treatment and sludge dewatering — in a standard ISO container footprint. All pipework, electrical, instrumentation and control is assembled and factory-tested before dispatch.

  **Commissioning:** Days to a few weeks from delivery, vs months for conventional civil construction. Requires a prepared base and utility connections (power, water inlet, discharge).

  **Container options:** 20 ft and 40 ft ISO standard dimensions; units can be combined for larger flow rates or additional treatment stages.

  **Relocation:** The container can be disconnected and moved as operational needs change — useful as temporary capacity during planned maintenance of a permanent plant, or when a site's purpose changes.

  **Control and monitoring:** SCADA/HMI with remote access included; operators can monitor and adjust all treatment stages from off-site without permanent staffing.

  **Reference case — Irbe Fish processing facility:**
  - Application: Canned fish production, discharge to sea
  - Flow: 200 m³/day (10 m³/h)
  - Result: Inlet TSS >1 800 mg/l reduced to <3 mg/l; inlet COD 2 600–3 200 mg/l reduced to 18 mg/l

Some sites don't have months for civil works, and some don't have a permanent site at
all. Our containerized plants pack the full treatment line — screening, flotation,
biological treatment and dewatering — into a standard container footprint, assembled and
tested before it ever leaves the factory. What arrives on site is close to plug-and-play,
and what leaves, if the site's needs change, can be relocated rather than demolished.
