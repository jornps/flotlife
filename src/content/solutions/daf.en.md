technicalDetails: |
  **Operating principle:** Pressurized water saturated with dissolved air is released into the flotation tank, generating millions of microscopic bubbles (20–60 µm diameter). These attach to suspended solids, fats and oils, carrying them to the surface as a float layer. A mechanical drag skimmer removes the float as concentrated sludge; heavier solids settle and are discharged from the tank bottom.

  **Capacity range:** 2–500 m³/h per unit in a single above-grade configuration.

  **Construction:** Stainless steel AISI 304/316 or HDPE body; all wetted parts food-grade compliant. Skid-mounted for fast installation.

  **Standard features:**
  - Factory-tested before delivery
  - Recycle saturation system for efficient bubble generation
  - Adjustable effluent weir
  - Surface drag skimmer
  - Polymer dosing integration for enhanced phosphorus and fine-particle removal
  - Remote monitoring ready

  **Typical removal rates** (with coagulation/polymer pre-treatment):
  - Suspended solids (TSS): >95%
  - Fats, oils and grease (FOG): >90%
  - Phosphorus (P): up to 95%
  - BOD reduction: 40–70% (DAF alone; biological stage required for full BOD compliance)

Dissolved air flotation injects microscopic air bubbles into water, attaching to
suspended solids, fats and oils and carrying them to the surface, where they're
skimmed off as a concentrated sludge. It's compact, fast, and effective across a wide
range of loads — which is why it sits at the center of most of the treatment lines we
design, whether the water comes from a fish farm, a food processing plant, or a ship.
