Dissolved air flotation injects microscopic air bubbles into water, attaching to
suspended solids, fats and oils and carrying them to the surface, where they're
skimmed off as a concentrated sludge. It's compact, fast, and effective across a wide
range of loads — which is why it sits at the center of most of the treatment lines we
design, whether the water comes from a fish farm, a food processing plant, or a ship.
